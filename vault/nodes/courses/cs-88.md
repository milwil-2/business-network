CS 88 covers the computational ideas of [[cs-61a]] through a data-science lens. It introduces programming in Python, abstraction, higher-order functions, and recursion, while motivating each idea with tabular data and real datasets.

The course is paired with the Data 8 sequence as a programming-focused companion, giving data-science students the computational structures they need without the full breadth of the EECS introductory course. Topics include functions, data abstraction, mutability, and an introduction to SQL.

Together with [[data-8]], CS 88 serves as a prerequisite for [[data-100]], bridging foundational data science into its principles-and-techniques sequel.
