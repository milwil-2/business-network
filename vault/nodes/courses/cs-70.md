CS 70 develops the mathematical maturity that the rest of the CS curriculum assumes. The first half covers proofs and discrete structures — induction, modular arithmetic, [[graph-theory]], and counting — while the second half builds discrete and continuous probability from the ground up.

Students learn proof by induction (a close cousin of [[recursion]]), reason about [[dag]] structures, and apply modular arithmetic to cryptography and error-correcting codes. The probability half introduces random variables, expectation, concentration inequalities, and Markov chains.

CS 70 is a prerequisite for the theory- and probability-heavy courses ahead, including [[cs-170]], [[cs-161]], [[cs-188]], and [[cs-189]].
