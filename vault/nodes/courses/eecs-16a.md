EECS 16A introduces electrical engineering and computer sciences through the unifying lens of linear algebra. Students learn to model real systems — tomographic imaging, GPS-style localization, and touchscreen sensing — as systems of linear equations.

The course develops vectors, matrices, Gaussian elimination, null spaces, and least squares, then applies them to circuit analysis with resistive networks and op-amps. Labs reinforce the theory by building working sensing and measurement devices.

As the first half of the EECS lower-division systems sequence, EECS 16A is the direct prerequisite for [[eecs-16b]], where the linear-algebra toolkit extends into dynamical systems and control.
