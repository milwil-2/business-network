CS 162 examines the abstractions an operating system provides — processes, threads, address spaces, and files — and how they are implemented. Students build a real OS kernel (Pintos) over a series of demanding projects.

Major topics include concurrency and synchronization, CPU scheduling, virtual memory, and file systems. The memory hierarchy and [[caching]] ideas from [[cs-61c]] return here at the level of page caches, buffer caches, and replacement policies.

Building on the data structures of [[cs-61b]] and the architecture of [[cs-61c]], CS 162 is a cornerstone of the systems track, with deep ties to databases, networking, and security.
