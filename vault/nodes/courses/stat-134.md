Stat 134 is a rigorous, calculus-based introduction to probability. It begins with combinatorics and the axioms of probability, then develops conditional probability, independence, and Bayes' rule.

The heart of the course is random variables: discrete and continuous distributions, expectation and variance, joint distributions, covariance, and conditional expectation. It culminates in the major limit theorems — the law of large numbers and the central limit theorem — which provide the theoretical justification for [[hypothesis-testing]] and statistical inference.

Stat 134 supplies the probabilistic grounding for statistics, machine learning, and any field that reasons quantitatively about uncertainty.
