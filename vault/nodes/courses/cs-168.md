CS 168 explains how the Internet works as a layered system. It covers the design principles behind packet switching, the IP protocol, and the end-to-end argument that shaped the network's architecture.

Core topics include routing algorithms (which build on graph traversals), reliable transport and TCP congestion control, DNS, and content delivery. Students gain intuition for why the Internet scales and where it breaks under load.

Building on the systems foundation of [[cs-61c]], CS 168 complements the security material in [[cs-161]] and the operating-systems perspective of [[cs-162]].
