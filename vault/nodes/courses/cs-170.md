CS 170 is the upper-division algorithms course, organized around design paradigms: divide and conquer, graph algorithms, greedy algorithms, [[dynamic-programming]], and linear programming. Each is developed with careful correctness proofs and [[big-o-notation]] analysis.

The graph-algorithms unit revisits [[bfs]], [[dfs]], [[topological-sort]] of a [[dag]], and shortest paths via [[dijkstra]] and Bellman-Ford, grounding everything in [[graph-theory]]. The course closes with NP-completeness, reductions, and approximation algorithms for intractable problems.

Drawing on the data structures of [[cs-61b]] and the discrete math and probability of [[cs-70]], CS 170 is the theoretical centerpiece of the major.
