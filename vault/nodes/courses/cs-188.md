CS 188 surveys the foundational ideas of artificial intelligence. It begins with search — uninformed methods like [[bfs]] and informed methods like [[a-star]] — then moves to adversarial game-playing with minimax and alpha-beta pruning.

The course then covers reasoning under uncertainty: Markov decision processes, reinforcement learning, Bayesian networks, and hidden Markov models. It closes with an introduction to machine learning, including [[neural-network]] models and the perceptron.

Resting on the data structures of [[cs-61b]] and the probability of [[cs-70]], CS 188 is the AI gateway course and a common precursor to deeper machine-learning study.
