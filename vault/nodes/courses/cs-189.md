CS 189 is the mathematically rigorous machine-learning course. It develops models from first principles, starting with [[linear-regression]] and logistic regression, fit by least squares and [[gradient-descent]], and grounded in linear algebra and probability.

The course covers regularization and the bias-variance tradeoff to combat [[overfitting]], support vector machines and kernels, decision trees and ensembles, and dimensionality reduction with PCA. Unsupervised methods include [[k-means-clustering]], and the final units develop [[neural-network]] models and backpropagation.

It draws heavily on the linear algebra of [[math-54]], the probability of [[cs-70]], and the signals-and-systems background of [[eecs-16b]], making it a capstone of the machine-learning track.
