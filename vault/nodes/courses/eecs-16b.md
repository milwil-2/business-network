EECS 16B continues the systems sequence, moving from static linear systems to dynamics over time. Students study linear differential equations, discrete- and continuous-time systems, stability, and feedback control, motivated by building a robotic car.

Later units cover the singular value decomposition, principal component analysis, and an introduction to classification — the linear-algebraic foundations of machine learning. Circuit topics expand to include transistors, filters, and energy storage.

Building on [[eecs-16a]] and the math of [[math-54]], EECS 16B is a prerequisite for [[cs-189]], supplying the SVD, PCA, and signals background that the machine-learning course assumes.
