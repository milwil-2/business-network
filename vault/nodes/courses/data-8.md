Data 8 is the foundational data science course, blending three perspectives: computing, statistical inference, and domain context. Students program in Python with tables and visualizations to explore real datasets from the first week.

The course builds inference from resampling: it teaches [[hypothesis-testing]] through permutation tests, confidence intervals via the bootstrap, and prediction with simple [[linear-regression]]. The emphasis is on understanding uncertainty rather than memorizing formulas.

As the entry point to the data science major, Data 8 is a prerequisite for [[data-100]] and equips students from every discipline with a working toolkit for data-driven reasoning.
