CS 161 teaches how to reason about security from an adversary's perspective. It opens with memory-safety vulnerabilities — buffer overflows and the exploits they enable — building directly on the systems knowledge from [[cs-61c]].

The course then covers cryptography (symmetric and public-key encryption, hashing, signatures), web security (XSS, CSRF, SQL injection), and network security including TLS and firewalls. Throughout, it emphasizes threat modeling and the principle of designing systems to fail safely.

Drawing on the probability and number theory from [[cs-70]] and the architecture from [[cs-61c]], CS 161 connects to networking topics in [[cs-168]] and rounds out the systems track.
